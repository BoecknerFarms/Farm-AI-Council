## Final Recommendation

**1. Final Recommendation:**  
Avoid herbicides with known phytotoxicity to sunflowers or beans (e.g., glyphosate in late growth stages, 2,4-D on sunflowers). Prioritize selective herbicides with short residual activity (e.g., S-metolachlor) for pre-emergence use, and avoid residual herbicides with long soil persistence (e.g., chlorimuron) if crops are in rotation.  

**2. Why:**  
- **Compatibility:** Sunflowers and beans have distinct herbicide sensitivities. Glyphosate or 2,4-D risk damage if applied during flowering or late growth stages. Beans may tolerate some herbicides but are vulnerable post-flowering.  
- **Weed Management:** Without weed species data, broad-spectrum herbicides risk non-selective injury. Pre-emergence herbicides reduce crop damage risks.  
- **Rotation Risks:** Residual herbicides from prior crops (e.g., sulfonylureas) could harm sunflowers or beans.
